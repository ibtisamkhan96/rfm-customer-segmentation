- 500,000+ transactions cleaned to UK customers only
- Columns: Invoice, StockCode, Description, Quantity, InvoiceDate, Price, CustomerID, Country

## What is RFM?
RFM is a proven customer segmentation framework used by retail and ecommerce analysts worldwide:

| Dimension | What it measures | Score |
|---|---|---|
| Recency (R) | How recently did the customer buy? | 1-4 (4 = most recent) |
| Frequency (F) | How many times have they bought? | 1-4 (4 = most frequent) |
| Monetary (M) | How much have they spent in total? | 1-4 (4 = highest value) |

Each customer gets an RFM score combining all three dimensions, then assigned to a named segment.

## Customer Segments

| Segment | Description | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Champion | Bought recently, often, and high value | Reward and retain — ask for referrals |
| Loyal | Buy consistently | Upsell complementary products |
| Potential Loyal | Recent with growing frequency | Increase purchase frequency |
| New Customer | Just bought for the first time | Onboard well — first 30 days critical |
| At Risk | Used to buy often but gone quiet | Win-back campaign immediately |
| Cannot Lose | Highest frequency but not buying recently | High-touch personal outreach |
| Hibernating | Low engagement across the board | Low-cost reactivation email |
| Lost | Bought once, long time ago | Generic campaigns only |

## Key Findings
- Champion customers represent a small percentage of the base but drive a disproportionate share of revenue
- At Risk customers represent recoverable revenue — they liked the product and just need a reason to return
- Month 1 retention is the single most important metric — converting first-time buyers to second-time buyers dramatically increases predicted LTV
- Cannot Lose customers (historically high frequency, recently gone quiet) need immediate high-touch outreach

## The Cohort Heatmap
The most visually powerful chart in this project. Each row is a monthly cohort of customers. Each column shows what percentage of that cohort is still active N months later. Darker colours indicate lower retention. This chart immediately reveals whether the business is getting better or worse at retaining customers over time.
